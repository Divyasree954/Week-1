RECIPE

CHICKEN CURRY

How to make Chicken Curry Simply(stepwise)

Ingerdients

  • oil
  • coconut milk
  • turmeric powder
  • water
  • Chicken
  • 1 small bay leaf
  • 2 inch cinnamon piece
  • 4 cloves
  • 3 green cardomoms
  • Green chillies
  • Ginger&garlic paste
  • 1 big tomato
  • 2 Onions
  • yogurt
  • curry leaves
  • chilli powder
  • coriander powder
  • garam masala
  • salt
  • coriander leaves

Steps to Prepare Chicken Curry

  1. Heat up oil and add all the spices (cinnamon, mustard seeds, cardamom, star anise, cloves )... add Ginger garlic paste and keep mix for 30 second....
  2. add onion and sorte until turn light brown. add tomato, chili and few curry leaves... mix until tomatoes cook abit...
  3. once the tomatoes cook abit, add chicken pieces and mix well. mix the chicken time to time and cover until halfly cook.
  4. No need to add water because chicken will produce water... do not use high flame ... medium flame is good enough
  5. once the chicken cook (half cook), add powders (turmeric powder, chili powder, chicken curry powder) mix well and can add more curry leaves... (optional)
  6. cover and keep mix time to time...and cover again.. this to make sure the powders cooked well...if not it will give raw smell.. No need to add water because chicken will produce water...
  7. after the gravy cook well, and the raw smell if powder disappear, add a bowl of water.. if want more gravy or curry can add another 1/2 bowl. mix well and cover it for 3-5 minutes..
  8. uncover and mix well.. the chicken will be already cook well in this stage... when we did not cover, the curry or gravy will start to get thick.... cook till the consistency of curry you want... - some like abit watery and some like it to be thick ....so depends to you....
  9. once it perfect consistency, add salt to taste and add coconut milk.. amount of coconut milk also depends to you...but don't put too much... some don't like to add coconut milk, it's OK.... it's jz a optional.. once add coconut milk the gravy will start to be more thicker...
  10. mix for 1 minute and it's ready... taste abit to make sure the salt is enough...then transfer it to serving bowl.
  11. Serve It and Enjoy... Best to eat with white rice, or briyani rice chappati naan bread white bread poori dosa, idli.

Cooking Terms

Marinate:

To soak the chicken pieces in a mixture of yogurt, spices, and other flavorings to tenderize and infuse them with flavor before cooking.

Saute:

To cook the chicken, onions, and other aromatics in oil or ghee over medium heat until they are lightly browned and fragrant.

Simmer

To cook the chicken in the curry sauce over low heat, allowing the flavors to meld together and the chicken to become tender.

Reduce

To cook the curry sauce over medium to high heat to evaporate excess liquid and concentrate the flavors, resulting in a thicker, more intense sauce.

Fry:

To cook ingredients in hot oil until they are crisp or golden brown. Some curry recipes call for frying the chicken pieces before adding them to the curry sauce to add extra flavor and texture.

Garnish:

To add decorative and flavorful elements to a dish before serving. Common garnishes for chicken curry include chopped cilantro, sliced green onions, or a drizzle of yogurt.
